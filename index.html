Abstract

We present RMMI, a whole-body task-space reactive mobile manipulator controller capable of reaching desired end effector poses while avoiding static obstacles. Our work focuses on collision avoidance within a holistic reactive controller; allowing control of the robot inside confined spaces such as a bookshelf. Our controller is formulated as a Quadratic Program (QP) that solves for joint velocities required to achieve a target spatial velocity. We propose using a neural signed distance field (SDF) to obtain the robot's distances from the scene. To represent the robot, we sample points on its mesh, allowing a more precise representation of the robot's outer geometry in contrast to using geometric primitives. We formulate the collision avoidance inside this controller as inequality constraints for the points representing the robot. In addition of a collision cost, formulated as an average of the distance across the points representing the robot. We evaluate our approach in a simulated reaching task, outperforming previous methods, that relies on representing both the robot and the scene as a set of primitive geometries. Furthermore, we tested our approach on a real-world platform, in which the robot reached inside a bookshelf and a cluttered table. Our approach is capable of reaching a target pose in clutter or confined spaces within a holistic reactive controller.
